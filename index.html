5 Understanding Proposals

When Claude makes changes to your code, it doesn't change your live app directly. Instead, it creates a proposal (technically called a "pull request" in GitHub).

Think of it like this: your app has a main version that everyone sees. When Claude writes new code, it creates a separate draft version. You can review this draft, see a live preview of how it looks, and decide whether to accept it.

Why this matters:

  • Safety net: If something goes wrong, your live app is unaffected
  • Preview first: Vercel automatically creates a preview URL so you can test changes before they go live
  • Easy iteration: Don't like something? Give Claude feedback and it updates the proposal
  • History: Every change is tracked, so you can always go back if needed

The review process:

  1. Claude creates a proposal with its changes
  2. You'll see a link to the proposal on GitHub
  3. Vercel automatically builds a preview (look for the preview link in the proposal)
  4. Test the preview‚Äîclick around, make sure it works how you want
  5. If you want changes, tell Claude what to fix (it will update the same proposal)
  6. When you're happy, click "Merge" on GitHub to make it live

Don't worry if this sounds complicated‚ÄîClaude will guide you through it, and you'll get the hang of it after your first project.

7 Add to Your Home Screen

Once your app is deployed, you can add it to your phone like a native app.

On iPhone (Safari):

  1. Open your app's URL in Safari
  2. Tap the Share button (square with arrow)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Give it a name and tap "Add"

On Android (Chrome):

  1. Open your app's URL in Chrome
  2. Tap the three-dot menu
  3. Tap "Add to Home screen"
  4. Confirm the name and tap "Add"

Your app now appears alongside your other apps!
